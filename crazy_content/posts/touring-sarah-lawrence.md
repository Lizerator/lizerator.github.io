# NYC Subway System Review and Rating

If there is one thing I love it would definitely be public transit, followed by women who have equally shitty music taste to mine. Over my three or so days in NYC I used the subway system plenty of times, at first it was comiplacted and confsing but by the end I kinda figured it out. This review is pretty much limited to Manhattan since that is where I spent most of my time. The rest of this review relies on the following image.

[!A map of the Manhattan subway system](https://pyxis.nymag.com/v1/imgs/2dc/39a/21303d5876ba58a1e2413ea1d929995fe9-26-subway-map-1.2x.w710.jpg)

Most movement in NYC is made up and down the island, Uptown refering to the direction twoards Harlem (on this map up) and Downtown refering to the direction twoards the World Trade Center (on this map down). Upon begining any trip you must figure out whether your destionation is uptown or downtown from your current position. An easy way to do this is use the street numbers. Much of the island is cut across by streets number one through like a zillion, one being the most downtown street. If your destination is 150th street(Harlem) and you are at 47th street (Times Sq) you must take a train way Uptown. For most of the island the trains running this way are the 1 2 and 3 on the west side and the 4 5 and 6 on the east side. After a little use you gain the ability to navigate pretty much anywhere after being given a street number or intersection.

Compared to BART, the NYC Subway has some great variety in station design among the different buroughs. One of the most suprising thinkgs was when getting into Jamacia station after getting off the Airport Skytram I came to realize that the underground portion of the station has the exact same brickwork and colors as E building. It was scarily similar, even down to the weird benches with slats on them.

Most stations in Manhattan have a similar design of white tile but their stations almost seem like maze's underground. Thin tunnels with barely enough room to stand between the wall and the track. It gives a very cool feel having everything so cramped, theirs also signage everywhere to make scheduels and directions of trains known which really keeps you immersed in the experience.

The trains themselves are sadly quite boring. Most of the ones I got to ride just had seating facing inward along each wall of the subway, probabaly to maximize standing room. Few trains had futuristic screens like the BART has, knowing which stop was coming up required reading a simple map of that particular line and counting stops. This was very easy once you get used to it.

My final point to make about the subway is the existince of express trains. Every line has a logo, a letter or number inside a colored circle, but some trains have a diamond rather than a circle. These trains are known as express trains and only stop every couple stops. The ammount of times I boarded an express train expecting it to be a local train isn't very high but it is always embarassing. You have to get off at the next stop and wait for the same train in the opposite direction. I saw many tourists do it multiple times (sadly me included) but locals didn't make that mistake from what I saw.
### Overall Rating: 10/10
